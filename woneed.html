Program Overview

The WO-NEED Programme emerges as a beacon of hope, a targeted intervention designed to dismantle barriers and unlock the immense economic potential of rural women across the State.

This initiative is more than just a program; it's a strategic investment in the future of our communities, recognizing that empowering women is synonymous with driving sustainable economic development.

Program Objectives

Financial Inclusion

Providing access to microloans, savings programs, and financial literacy training for rural women.

Business Training

Comprehensive skills development in business management, record-keeping, and product development.

Market Access

Creating linkages to markets and enhancing value chains for women's products and services.

Community Support

Building women's cooperatives and peer support networks for sustainable empowerment.

Agriculture Support

Training in sustainable farming techniques and agribusiness for rural women farmers.

Program Details

Program Structure

WO-NEED operates through a phased approach:

  1. Phase 1 (3 months): Community mobilization and needs assessment
  2. Phase 2 (6 months): Core training and cooperative formation
  3. Phase 3 (3 months): Business launch with seed funding
  4. Phase 4 (Ongoing): Mentorship and market linkage support

Eligibility Criteria

To participate in WO-NEED, women must:

  • Be residents of rural Cross River State communities
  • Be aged 18 years and above
  • Demonstrate interest in starting or expanding a business
  • Commit to program participation and group activities
  • Have endorsement from community leaders

Program Benefits

WO-NEED participants receive:

  • Access to interest-free microloans
  • Comprehensive business training
  • Formation into cooperative societies
  • Market access and linkage support
  • Agricultural inputs and training
  • Mentorship from successful women entrepreneurs
  • Basic literacy and numeracy classes (where needed)

Focus Areas

WO-NEED focuses on key economic activities for rural women:

Agribusiness

  • Food processing
  • Small-scale farming
  • Animal husbandry

Handicrafts

  • Weaving
  • Pottery
  • Beadwork

Retail Services

  • Community shops
  • Mobile markets
  • Food vending

Community Services

  • Childcare
  • Elderly care
  • Community catering
