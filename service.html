Bank Of Industry (BOI) Fund

The Bank of Industry fund is a collaborative matching fund between the Cross River State Government and the Bank of Industry on a 50/50 basis. The fund aligns with MEDA’s goals of facilitating the establishment and growth of MSMEs in the state. The total fund amount is N500,000,000.00. This fund is not limited to any particular sector; rather, it is accessible to any indigenous entrepreneur looking to grow their business.

Requirements

  • ✓ Farm registration with state agriculture department
  • ✓ Land ownership documents/lease agreement (min. 3 years)
  • ✓ Detailed agribusiness proposal with market analysis
  • ✓ Group membership (for cooperatives) with 10+ members
  • ✓ Two guarantors with verifiable income
  • ✓ Environmental impact assessment (for large-scale projects)

Bank Of Agriculture

The Bank of Agriculture fund is a microcredit initiative established by the Agency in collaboration with the Bank of Agriculture on a 50/50 basis. It supports microenterprises across Cross River State. The global fund amount is N200,000,000.00. This initiative focuses on supporting microenterprises in selected sectors, providing much-needed financial assistance to drive economic activities and foster growth in these areas.

Requirements

  • ✓ Farm registration with state agriculture department
  • ✓ Land ownership documents/lease agreement (min. 3 years)
  • ✓ Detailed agribusiness proposal with market analysis
  • ✓ Group membership (for cooperatives) with 10+ members
  • ✓ Two guarantors with verifiable income
  • ✓ Environmental impact assessment (for large-scale projects)

CRS MSME DEV. FUND

The EDC Support Fund is a funding window created by the Agency to assist graduates from the South-South Entrepreneurship Development Centre (SSEDC). This initiative is a 100-million-naira revolving MSME credit loan fund with an interest rate of 9% per annum. The fund is categorized into three parts, providing tailored support to ensure that graduates of SSEDC have the financial backing needed to start or expand their ventures.

Requirements

  • ✓ Completed EDC training program certification
  • ✓ Business must be registered (BN or CAC)
  • ✓ 6 months bank statements (business/personal)
  • ✓ Three customer references with contact details
  • ✓ Evidence of existing market demand (orders/contracts)
  • ✓ Personal equity contribution (minimum 10% of request)
Apply Now

MEDA Micro Credit (MMC) Fund

The MEDA Micro Credit Scheme is designed to support youth-owned microbusinesses in Cross River State. This initiative is a collaboration between MEDA and the Ministry of Youth and Sports Development. It provides financial assistance to empower young entrepreneurs, encouraging business growth and sustainability. The scheme addresses the funding gap for youth enterprises, contributing to economic development and job creation within the state.

Requirements

  • ✓ Active business for at least 6 months
  • ✓ Membership in recognized business association
  • ✓ Two passport photographs
  • ✓ Utility bill (not older than 3 months)
  • ✓ Group guarantee (5-10 members cross-guaranteeing)
  • ✓ Weekly repayment capacity demonstrated
