Program Overview

The Enterprise Club Project is a transformative initiative designed to cultivate a vibrant culture of entrepreneurship within the heart of the State's secondary schools, spanning all 18 local government areas.

We believe entrepreneurial thinking is a vital life skill that fosters creativity, problem-solving, and resilience. This project aims to ignite that spark in young students, equipping them to become the innovators and business leaders of tomorrow.

Program Objectives

Entrepreneurial Fundamentals

Early introduction to business concepts and creative thinking.

Practical Skills

Hands-on training in product development and basic business operations.

Mentorship

Guidance from successful entrepreneurs and business leaders.

Technology Exposure

Introduction to digital tools and innovative technologies.

Financial Literacy

Basic money management and financial responsibility training.

Program Details

Program Structure

Enterprise Clubs operate through:

  1. Weekly Club Meetings: After-school sessions with structured curriculum
  2. Quarterly Workshops: Intensive skill-building events
  3. Annual Competitions: Business idea challenges with prizes
  4. Mentorship Pairings: Connecting students with local entrepreneurs

Eligibility Criteria

To join an Enterprise Club, students must:

  • Be enrolled in a participating secondary school
  • Maintain satisfactory academic performance
  • Demonstrate interest in entrepreneurship
  • Have consent from participants institution
  • Commit to regular club attendance

Program Benefits

Enterprise Club members gain:

  • Early exposure to business concepts
  • Hands-on entrepreneurial experience
  • Mentorship from successful business owners
  • Access to mini-grants for student ventures
  • Participation certificates
  • Networking with like-minded peers
