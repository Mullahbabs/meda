Established to address the challenges faced by small businesses and underserved communities, MEDA provides innovative financial solutions, capacity-building programs, and strategic support to enable sustainable development..

Our Objectives

The Foundation On Which We Stand

Promoting Change

Promoting a change in the perception and general attitude of Cross Riverians, re-orienting them towards private enterprise by encouraging the entrepreneurial spirit and a deliberate movement away from looking for paid employment.


Strenghtening Infrastructure

Infrastructure for MSME, building technical & managerial capacity for domestic MSMEs through strategic collaborations and partnerships with stakeholders, ensuring long-term growth and sustainability of small businesses.

Access To Finance

Enhancing MSME access to finance through various schemes undertaken by the government and selected financial institutions. These programs aim to bridge the funding gap and empower entrepreneurs across various sectors.

Facilitating Linkages

Facilitating linkages with key business associations such as MAN, NASSI, NASME, and CALCCIMA to maximize the opportunities and benefits that come with such partnerships, fostering economic growth and development within the state.

The Director General

DISTINGUISHED STAKEHOLDERS, PARTNERS, & THE VIBRANT BUSINESS COMMUNITY OF CROSS RIVER STATE

Upon assuming office, His Excellency, Senator Prince Bassey Edet Otu, the Executive Governor of Cross River State, articulated the "People First" mandate. This people-centric vision ensures that every policy directly benefits Cross Riverians. Recognizing the pivotal role of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME) development, emphasized entrepreneurship at all levels, which led to the creation of the People Oriented Initiatives for Skills and Enterprise Development (POISED) project. This comprehensive initiative encompasses the Retiree Entrepreneurship Development Initiative (REDI), Women Neighbourhood Economic and Entrepreneurship Development (WO-NEED), Youth Local Entrepreneurship Acceleration Program (YOU-LEAP), Campus CEO, Enterprise Clubs & Competitions.

As the Director-General of the Cross River State Microfinance and Enterprise Development Agency(MEDA), I am honored to highlight that we have made significant strides in fostering the growth and development of MSMEs within our state, aligning with the Governor's vision through the POISED program. We have embraced this mandate, ensuring no one is left behind and igniting the spirit of enterprise across board.
At MEDA, we understand that the success of our MSMEs is intrinsically linked to the prosperity of our state. Therefore we remain dedicated to providing the necessary support & resources to empower our entrepreneurs to achieve their full potential. Together, we are building a stronger, more resilient, and prosperous Cross River State

MEDA Partner Funded Programs

BOI FUND

All Access


The Bank of Industry fund is a collaborative matching fund between the Cross River State Government and the Bank of Industry on a 50/50 basis. The fund aligns with MEDAâ€™s goals of facilitating the establishment and growth of MSMEs in the state. The total fund amount is N500,000,000.00. This fund is not limited to any particular sector; rather, it is accessible to any indigenous entrepreneur looking to grow their business.

BANK OF AGRICULTURE

Farmers And Agric Bodies


The Bank of Agriculture fund is a microcredit initiative established by the Agency in collaboration with the Bank of Agriculture on a 50/50 basis. It supports microenterprises across Cross River State. The global fund amount is N200,000,000.00. This initiative focuses on supporting microenterprises in selected sectors, providing much-needed financial assistance to drive economic activities and foster growth in these areas.

Apply

EDC SUPPORT FUND

Accessible To All


The EDC Support Fund is a funding window created by the Agency to assist graduates from the South-South Entrepreneurship Development Centre (SSEDC). This initiative is a 100-million-naira revolving MSME credit loan fund with an interest rate of 9% per annum. The fund is categorized into three parts, providing tailored support to ensure that graduates of SSEDC have the financial backing needed to start or expand their ventures.

Apply

MEDA MICRO-CREDIT

Accessible To All


The MEDA Micro Credit Scheme is designed to support youth-owned microbusinesses in Cross River State. This initiative is a collaboration between MEDA and the Ministry of Youth and Sports Development. It provides financial assistance to empower young entrepreneurs, encouraging business growth and sustainability. The scheme addresses the funding gap for youth enterprises, contributing to economic development and job creation within the state.
