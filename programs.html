People Oriented Initiatives for Skills & Enterprise Development (POISED)

The People First Initiatives for Skills and Enterprise Development (POISED Project) is a comprehensive program fostering widespread economic empowerment in Cross River State. Strategically integrating Campus CEO, Enterprise Clubs & Competition, WO-NEED, YOU-LEAP, and REDI, POISED is a significant stride towards a more vibrant and economically empowered Cross River. This initiative directly reflects the 'People-First' agenda of His Excellency, Sen. Prince Bassey Edet Otu, the Executive Governor of Cross River state.

Programs Under The Poised Initiative

  • • Retiree Entrepreneurship Development Initiative (REDI)
  • • Women Neighbourhood Economic & Entrepreneurship Development (WO-NEED)
  • • Youth Local Entrepreneurship Acceleration (YOU-LEEP)
  • • Campus CEO
  • • Enterprise Clubs

Agri-Business Development & Empowerment Programme

The Agricultural Development and Empowerment Programme (ADEP) is a transformative, three-year initiative strategically designed to unlock the untapped potential of youth and women in Cross River State through structured and sustainable agribusiness activities. Spanning from 2024 to 2027, ADEP is more than just a program; it's a comprehensive strategy to build resilient communities, drive economic diversification, and foster a culture of entrepreneurship within the agricultural sector.

ADEP Objectives

  • • Enhancing Livelihood
  • • Promoting Sustainable Agricultural Practices
  • • Fostering Entrepreneurship
  • • Ensuring Economic Growth

Creative Accelerator Programme (CAP)

Cross River State, a region brimming with artistic talent and cultural vibrancy, is poised to become a thriving hub for the creative economy. However, many of its talented creatives face significant challenges: limited access to essential skills, a lack of mentorship, restricted market access, and insufficient funding opportunities. To address these critical needs and unlock the immense potential of the state's creative sector, the Creative Accelerator Programme (CAP) has been strategically designed.

Applicable Fundings

BOI FUND

All Access


The Bank of Industry fund is a collaborative matching fund between the Cross River State Government and the Bank of Industry on a 50/50 basis. The fund aligns with MEDA’s goals of facilitating the establishment and growth of MSMEs in the state. The total fund amount is N500,000,000.00. This fund is not limited to any particular sector; rather, it is accessible to any indigenous entrepreneur looking to grow their business.

Read More

BANK OF AGRICULTURE

Farmers And Agric Bodies


The Bank of Agriculture fund is a microcredit initiative established by the Agency in collaboration with the Bank of Agriculture on a 50/50 basis. It supports microenterprises across Cross River State. The global fund amount is N200,000,000.00. This initiative focuses on supporting microenterprises in selected sectors, providing much-needed financial assistance to drive economic activities and foster growth in these areas.

Read More

EDC SUPPORT FUND

Accessible To All


The EDC Support Fund is a funding window created by the Agency to assist graduates from the South-South Entrepreneurship Development Centre (SSEDC). This initiative is a 100-million-naira revolving MSME credit loan fund with an interest rate of 9% per annum. The fund is categorized into three parts, providing tailored support to ensure that graduates of SSEDC have the financial backing needed to start or expand their ventures.

Read More

MEDA MICRO-CREDIT

Accessible To All


The MEDA Micro Credit Scheme is designed to support youth-owned microbusinesses in Cross River State. This initiative is a collaboration between MEDA and the Ministry of Youth and Sports Development. It provides financial assistance to empower young entrepreneurs, encouraging business growth and sustainability. The scheme addresses the funding gap for youth enterprises, contributing to economic development and job creation within the state.
