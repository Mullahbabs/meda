Programs

CAMPUS CEO

The Campus CEO Programme is an initiative of the Cross River State Microfinance and Enterprise Development Agency (MEDA) designed to foster entrepreneurial skills and innovation among university undergraduates in Cross River State. By providing access to mentorship, resources, and a supportive ecosystem, the programme aims to cultivate a new generation of entrepreneurs who can launch successful startups while still in university. The programme is structured to include awareness campaigns, capacity-building workshops, and a startup incubation component.

Target Audience: University Undergraduates

Key Components: Awareness, Capacity Building, Startup Incubation, Partnership Collaborations & Funding

ADEP

agri business development and empowerment programme

The Agri-business Development and Empowerment Programme (ADEP) is a three-year initiative designed to empower youths and women in Cross River State through structured agribusiness activities. Running from 2024 to 2027, ADEP aims to enhance livelihoods by promoting sustainable agricultural practices, fostering entrepreneurship, and ensuring economic growth. Beneficiaries will be clustered into value chains and form cooperatives, ensuring a collective approach to agribusiness development.

Target Audience: Youths & Women

Key Components: Economic Empowerment, Job Creation, Food Security, Capacity Building and Economic Growth.

DIGIGAP

Calabar DIGIGAP Program

In today’s rapidly evolving global economy, digital and technological skills are essential for economic participation and growth. Nigeria, with its young and dynamic population, stands at a critical juncture where leveraging digital skills can significantly contribute to national development. Cross River State, though rich in cultural heritage and natural resources, lags behind in technological advancements. The lack of widespread digital literacy and access to technology hinders the state’s youths from competing effectively in the modern job market. This programme seeks to address the digital skills gap by implementing a comprehensive training programme designed to equip 5,000 youths in Cross River State with the necessary digital and tech skills over a three-year period. In addition to tech training, the programme will incorporate entrepreneurship and business management skills to ensure that participants are not only employable but also capable of creating and sustaining their businesses.

Target Audience: Youths

Key Components: Tech, Entrepreneurship, Access to Market, Access to Funding.

CAP

The Creative Accelerator Program

The Creative Accelerator Program (CAP) is an initiative aimed at nurturing and empowering creatives in Cross River State, Nigeria. The program seeks to address the unique challenges faced by creatives in the region, such as limited access to funding, market opportunities, and mentorship. CAP is designed to equip participants with entrepreneurial skills, connect them with potential investors, and provide a supportive network that fosters growth and sustainability in their creative endeavours. The programme is facilitated by the Office of the SA to the President (ovp) on Digital and Creative Economy in partnership with the Microfinance and Enterprise Development Agency as the implementing agency.

Target Audience: Creatives

Key Components: Skill Enhancement, Market Access, Funding Opportunities, Mentorship & Networking.

REDI

Calabar REDI Program

In Nigeria, retirement often brings financial insecurity, leading many civil servants to falsify their ages to extend their tenure. This issue, coupled with the sudden loss of income, has resulted in high levels of depression and even early mortality among retirees. The Retiree Entrepreneurship Development Initiative (REDI) is designed to address these challenges by providing a structured programme to prepare civil and public servants in Cross River State for life after retirement. The initiative will focus on equipping participants with financial literacy, entrepreneurial skills, and the tools necessary to establish a steady source of income post-retirement.

Target Audience: Retirees, Pre-retirees in the Civil and Public Service

Key Components: Financial Security, Entrepreneurial skills, Mental Well-being, Sustainable Livelihood

Clubs

Enterprise Clubs

The Enterprise Club Project is an initiative aimed at fostering entrepreneurial thinking and skills among secondary school students across the 18 local governments in Cross River State. By introducing students to the fundamentals of entrepreneurship at an early age, the project seeks to create a generation of innovative thinkers and future business leaders who can contribute to the state's economic growth.

Target Audience: Secondary School Students (SS1-SS3)

Key Components: Awareness, Club formation, Business formation (project) and Funding

WO-NEED

WO-NEED Program

Women, especially in rural areas, are often marginalised when it comes to economic opportunities. In Cross River State, rural women are key contributors to the agricultural and informal sectors but face significant challenges in accessing financial resources, business training, and markets. Empowering these women is crucial for the economic development of the state. By providing targeted support, the Women Neighbourhood Economic and Entrepreneurship Development Programme aims to pull rural women out of poverty, fostering self-reliance and improving their quality of life.

Target Audience: Women especially in rural areas

Key Components: Capacity Building, Economic Empowerment, Poverty Reduction, Savings and Financial Inclusion

YOU-LEAP

YOU-LEAP Program

Cross River State faces significant challenges related to youth unemployment, restiveness, poverty, and increasing suicide rates among young people. The Youth Local Entrepreneurship and Acceleration Programme (YOU-LEAP) is designed to address these issues by equipping youths with the skills, resources, and opportunities they need to become successful entrepreneurs. By focusing on both rural and urban youth, YOU-LEAP aims to create sustainable economic opportunities, reduce poverty, and foster a culture of innovation and self-reliance among the younger generation.

Target Audience: Youths (18-45 years)

Key Components: Job Creation, Capacity Building, Access to Market

Applicable Fundings

BOI FUND

All Access


The Bank of Industry fund is a collaborative matching fund between the Cross River State Government and the Bank of Industry on a 50/50 basis. The fund aligns with MEDA’s goals of facilitating the establishment and growth of MSMEs in the state. The total fund amount is N500,000,000.00. This fund is not limited to any particular sector; rather, it is accessible to any indigenous entrepreneur looking to grow their business.

BANK OF AGRICULTURE

Farmers And Agric Bodies


The Bank of Agriculture fund is a microcredit initiative established by the Agency in collaboration with the Bank of Agriculture on a 50/50 basis. It supports microenterprises across Cross River State. The global fund amount is N200,000,000.00. This initiative focuses on supporting microenterprises in selected sectors, providing much-needed financial assistance to drive economic activities and foster growth in these areas.

Read More

EDC SUPPORT FUND

Accessible To All


The EDC Support Fund is a funding window created by the Agency to assist graduates from the South-South Entrepreneurship Development Centre (SSEDC). This initiative is a 100-million-naira revolving MSME credit loan fund with an interest rate of 9% per annum. The fund is categorized into three parts, providing tailored support to ensure that graduates of SSEDC have the financial backing needed to start or expand their ventures.

Read More

MEDA MICRO-CREDIT

Accessible To All


The MEDA Micro Credit Scheme is designed to support youth-owned microbusinesses in Cross River State. This initiative is a collaboration between MEDA and the Ministry of Youth and Sports Development. It provides financial assistance to empower young entrepreneurs, encouraging business growth and sustainability. The scheme addresses the funding gap for youth enterprises, contributing to economic development and job creation within the state.
