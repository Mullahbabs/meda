History & Background

15 Years Since Inception

MEDA was established to tackle the pressing economic challenges faced by small-scale entrepreneurs, particularly in underserved and rural areas. Recognizing the critical role of microfinance in economic development, the agency has implemented programs designed to provide affordable and accessible financial services to those who need them the most.

Over the years, MEDA has expanded its reach through partnerships with government institutions, private sector organizations, and development agencies. These collaborations have enabled MEDA to roll out impactful programs, such as enterprise development workshops, youth empowerment initiatives, and community-focused microfinance schemes.

With a team of dedicated professionals and a visionary leadership, MEDA continues to innovate and adapt to the evolving needs of entrepreneurs, ensuring that its services remain relevant and impactful. The agency remains steadfast in its mission to empower individuals, strengthen communities, and drive sustainable economic development across Cross River State and beyond.

Our Leadership

The Director General

Mr. Great Usetu Ogban

Great Ogban, the Director General of the Microfinance & Enterprise Development Agency (MEDA), is a visionary leader and dynamic professional with a passion for driving economic growth and fostering sustainable development. With a distinguished career in public service and entrepreneurship advocacy, he has consistently demonstrated his commitment to empowering individuals and transforming communities.

As the Director General of MEDA, Great Ogban has been instrumental in designing and implementing innovative microfinance solutions tailored to the needs of grassroots entrepreneurs, small business owners, and underserved populations. Under his leadership, MEDA has expanded its reach, providing accessible financial services, training, and mentorship to thousands of individuals, thereby fostering economic independence and resilience.

Before his appointment at MEDA, Great Ogban served as the Special Assistant to the Governor on Entrepreneurship Development in Cross River State. In this role, he spearheaded key initiatives that created opportunities for small businesses and startups, significantly contributing to the growth of the entrepreneurial ecosystem in the state.
